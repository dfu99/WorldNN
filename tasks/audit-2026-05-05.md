## D2. KSG estimator under-estimates by 30-100%

Sanity check: the KSG MI estimator at `src/worldnn/utils.py::estimate_mi_ksg`
applied to bivariate Gaussian samples (n=5000, k=3) returns:

| $\rho$ | analytic $I$ (nats) | our KSG | ratio |
|----|----|----|----|
| 0.0 | 0.000 | 0.000 | exact |
| 0.3 | 0.047 | 0.000 | 0% (returns clamped 0) |
| 0.6 | 0.223 | 0.000 | 0% (returns clamped 0) |
| 0.9 | 0.830 | 0.548 | 66% |

The estimator under-reports MI substantially in the medium-correlation
regime. The `max(0.0, mi)` clamp masks the actual (negative) raw output
that signals failed estimation. Two implications:

- *No paper-numerics risk.* Paper §5.7 uses linear-probe $R^2 \to$
  Gaussian-MI lower bound, NOT this KSG. The KSG is only used in the
  obj-014 chain-MI experiment which is not currently surfaced in the
  paper.
- *Code health risk.* The function is named generically and could be
  trusted by a future reader without realizing it under-estimates.

**Action:** Pin the current behavior in a unit test (queue T29) AND
add a docstring warning that flags the under-estimation in 2-D Gaussian
regime. Do not rewrite the estimator now (would invalidate obj-014's
already-published numbers if it's referenced).

## D18. Reviewer D q2 closed via signal-dep noise sensitivity

The last "open" reviewer question — Reviewer D q2: *"Real organisms use
signal-dependent noise. Your channel adds Gaussian noise. Does the
conclusion change?"* — addressed without retraining via a sensitivity
analysis on the linear-probe recon ceiling.

Two noise models on the same `RockPushMatter`:
- Gaussian: $y' = y + \sigma \cdot \mathcal{N}(0, I)$
- Signal-dep (Weber): $y' = y + c \cdot |y| \cdot \mathcal{N}(0, I)$

| coeff $c$ | $R^2$ Gaussian | $R^2$ Signal-dep |
|----|----|----|
| 0.05 | 0.81 | 0.87 |
| 0.10 | 0.72 | 0.79 |
| 0.30 | 0.45 | 0.57 |
| 0.50 | 0.27 | 0.42 |
| 1.00 | 0.11 | 0.21 |

Signal-dep is *strictly above* Gaussian at every $c$ — low-amplitude
channels emit proportionally less corruption, so the recon ceiling
*rises* under the more biologically-realistic noise model. When matched
on effective per-channel $\sigma$ (panel B of `obj029_signal_dep_noise.png`)
the two curves coincide within $\pm 0.05$ $R^2$ across $\sigma \in [0, 0.6]$.

**Conclusion:** the Gaussian channel is the *conservative* choice. The
information-theoretic argument in §5.7 is at least as strong under a
signal-dependent noise model. Added a 4-line paragraph to §7.5
("Noise model sensitivity") summarizing this.
